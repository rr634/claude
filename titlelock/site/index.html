The threat

Your home's title is a public document — and that's the problem.

Deeds are filed at the county. A fraudster only needs to forge a transfer, file it, and on paper your home now belongs to someone else. They borrow against it, sell it, or evict tenants — and you don't find out until the damage is done. Title insurance you bought at closing does not cover fraud that happens after.

TitleLock closes that gap by watching the record itself.

Forged deed transfers

A fake quitclaim deed moves your home into a stranger's name in minutes.

Stolen equity loans

Criminals take out a mortgage or HELOC against your property and vanish with the cash.

Vacant & vacation homes

Second homes, rentals, and inherited property are prime targets — no one's watching.

Slow discovery

Most owners learn of the fraud months later — through a foreclosure notice or a buyer at the door.

Questions

What you should know.

Doesn't my title insurance already cover this?
Owner's title insurance protects you against problems that existed before you bought the home. It does not cover fraud committed against your title after closing — which is exactly what home title theft is. TitleLock fills that gap by monitoring for new, fraudulent filings going forward.
Can TitleLock physically stop a fraudulent filing?
No service can prevent a criminal from submitting a forged document to the county. What TitleLock does is detect it almost immediately and help you respond — so a fraudulent filing is caught and challenged in hours or days, not discovered months later in a foreclosure notice.
What happens when you detect something suspicious?
You get an instant alert by email (and text on Defend and Portfolio plans) with details of exactly what was recorded. On attorney-backed plans, our real estate legal team helps you verify whether it's legitimate and, if not, dispute the filing and notify the county recorder.
How do you monitor my property?
We match your address to its parcel in the official county land records and snapshot the current, legitimate deed and title. From then on, every new document recorded against that parcel is checked against your baseline and scored for risk.
Is my personal information safe?
Yes. We use your address only to look up public land records, and your contact details only to send your alerts. We never sell your data. Property records themselves are already public — we simply watch them for you.
Can I cancel anytime?
Absolutely. There are no contracts. Cancel from your dashboard whenever you like and monitoring stops at the end of your billing period.
