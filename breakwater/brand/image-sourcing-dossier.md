## 0. Read this first — method, honesty, and limits

**What's verified:** Every URL in this dossier was surfaced by live web search and is a real, on-platform link. All sources are **free-for-commercial-use** platforms (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay).

**What I could *not* do in this environment (so I won't fake it):** the network policy here blocks me from *opening* the image pages (HTTP 403). That means I could **not**:
- read each image's **exact photographer name** off the page, or
- visually confirm every frame beyond what the page **title** describes.

So I have **not invented** photographer credits or "I saw it" descriptions. Where the platform requires no attribution (all three here), the photographer is creditable from the page but **not legally required**. Treat each entry as "search-verified link + title"; the final **open-the-page confirmation pass** (exact credit + visual QA) is the one step still owed — see §8 for how we close it.

**Counting note:** this is a curated set of **~31 hand-picked individual images** plus **~15 deep-pool collection links** (each holding dozens of on-brand options) = well over the 50–100 target once collections are expanded. I prioritized *verifiable accuracy over padding the count with links I can't stand behind.*

### ⚠️ The release nuance that actually matters for a capital-raising site
A royalty-free *copyright* license is **not** a model/property/trademark release. For **commercial promotion** (which a fund website is), images showing **identifiable people, recognizable private homes/architecture, branded yachts, or logos** can carry **right-of-publicity / property-release** exposure even when the stock license is "free." Practical rules for Breakwater:
- Prefer **no recognizable faces** (your brand is discreet anyway — this is a feature, not a limitation).
- Prefer **wide/aerial or under-construction** shots where no specific identifiable luxury home is the "star," **or** images explicitly marked property-released.
- Avoid anything that could imply a **real, identifiable estate is a Breakwater asset** — that ties to the same "representative, not actual assets" disclaimer already on the site.
- You're a real-estate attorney; this is a 10-minute clearance review per hero image, not a blocker.

### The honest ceiling of free stock
Free platforms get you **~70%** of the Blackstone/Starwood look — excellent for aerials, cranes, dusk skylines, textures, and secondary slots. The **top 30%** (a singular, art-directed waterfront-estate hero that looks *owned and bespoke*) usually comes from **paid or commissioned** sources — see **§6**.
