# Breakwater Capital Partners
## Acquisition Screening Box & Deal Screen

*The first risk control is the box. We decline anything outside it. This screen converts a raw distressed lead into a "pursue / pass" decision in under 30 minutes, before any underwriting time is spent.*

---

## How to use this screen

1. Run every lead through **Section A (Hard Filters)** first. **Any single "FAIL" = PASS on the deal.** No exceptions without principal sign-off and a documented reason.
2. If it clears the hard filters, score **Section B (Scored Criteria)**. Total the points.
3. Apply the **decision rule** at the bottom.
4. Only deals that clear A and score above threshold advance to full underwriting (the model) and an acquisition memorandum.

---

## Section A — Hard Filters (binary: PASS / FAIL)

| # | Filter | Pass condition | Fail condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | **Geography** | In a primary or secondary target submarket (waterfront / luxury corridor). | Outside target markets. |
| A2 | **Asset type** | Luxury residential (spec home, waterfront development, small luxury multi-unit). | Commercial, raw land with no vertical, non-luxury tract product. |
| A3 | **Completion at entry** | 50%–90% complete (permits in place, vertical construction underway). | <50% (entitlement/early risk) or >90% (thin spread, retail-priced). |
| A4 | **Finished value** | $2M–$15M projected finished value. | Outside range. |
| A5 | **Cost to complete** | $250K–$3M remaining. | Outside range / unquantifiable. |
| A6 | **Achievable control** | A viable path to control exists (note, pref, JV, DIL, foreclosure, REO). | No realistic path to control; uncooperative stack with no leverage. |
| A7 | **Title / lien resolvability** | Liens and title defects are identifiable and resolvable through our infrastructure. | Unresolvable title, fatal litigation, environmental, or structural defect. |
| A8 | **Completion spread** | ≥ 20% projected margin to all-in basis at base case. | < 20% margin / no spread after honest costs. |
| A9 | **Hold horizon** | Plausible exit within 6–18 months. | Multi-year entitlement, build-out, or litigation horizon. |

> **Any FAIL → PASS on the deal.** Document the reason and move on. Discipline at the top of the funnel is what protects capital and credibility.

---

## Section B — Scored Criteria (0–5 each; max 50)

Score each from 0 (poor) to 5 (excellent). These rank the deals that survive Section A.

| # | Criterion | What "5" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| B1 | **Completion spread depth** | 30%+ margin to all-in basis; wide, durable cushion. |
| B2 | **Seller / counterparty motivation** | Lender wants it off the books now; estate/receiver under time pressure; cooperative. |
| B3 | **Control certainty & speed** | Clean path to title in weeks (DIL, note with cooperative borrower, REO). |
| B4 | **Construction risk** | Mostly cosmetic/finish work remaining; reputable original build; clear scope. |
| B5 | **Cost-to-complete confidence** | Independently verified budget; tight scope; low overrun risk. |
| B6 | **Submarket strength & liquidity** | Prime waterfront block with active, deep buyer demand. |
| B7 | **Exit clarity** | Obvious finished comp set; clear pricing; multiple exit paths (sale or refi). |
| B8 | **Capital efficiency** | Modest equity check relative to spread; attractive leverage available. |
| B9 | **Basis margin of safety** | All-in basis well below replacement cost / land+vertical value. |
| B10 | **Strategic / relationship value** | Strengthens a key lender/receiver relationship; seeds future deal flow. |

## Decision Rule

| Outcome | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 **PURSUE** | Clears all of Section A **and** scores **≥ 38 / 50** | Advance to full underwriting + acquisition memo. Begin control negotiations. |
| 🟡 **CONDITIONAL** | Clears Section A **and** scores **30–37** | Pursue only if a specific weakness can be fixed (price, structure, scope). Principal decision. |
| 🔴 **PASS** | Any Section A FAIL **or** score **< 30** | Decline. Log in the funnel for relationship continuity; revisit only on materially changed terms. |
