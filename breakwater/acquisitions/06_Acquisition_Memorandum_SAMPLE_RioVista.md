## 1. Executive Summary

A reputable local builder began a ~5,800 SF deep-water spec home on a prime Rio Vista block and reached roughly 75% completion — structure, envelope, and rough MEP complete — before a 24-month construction loan hit **maturity default** with no takeout and the budget exhausted by cost inflation. The senior lender (a regional bank's special-assets desk) wants the non-performing asset **off its books before quarter-end** and has offered to sell its note at a discount to face. The builder is cooperative and underwater on equity; two mechanic's liens cloud title.

This is a clean fit for the Breakwater box: 50–90% complete, $2–15M finished value, $250K–$3M to finish, 6–18 month hold. The value-creation thesis is the **completion spread** — we acquire the note at $3.70M (vs. $4.10M face), take title efficiently via deed-in-lieu with a foreclosure/credit-bid fallback, resolve the liens in-house, finish to CO on a verified ~$1.4M budget, and sell into a supply-constrained Rio Vista market at ~$7.5M.

This is a Breakwater deal specifically because three of our integrated capabilities are exactly what the situation requires: **in-house legal** (note assignment, DIL, foreclosure fallback), **in-house title** (the two liens are the gating risk, and we diligence/clear them before close), and **in-house brokerage** (proprietary lead and a real-time read on the finished comp set).

**The ask:** $1,649,000 of equity alongside a $4.8M acquisition/completion facility, offered as preferred equity at a 9% pref with an 80/20 promote, targeting ~1.41x / ~41% gross over 12 months.

## 2. The Situation

- **What broke and why.** A 24-month construction loan reached **maturity default**; materials/labor inflation pushed the job over budget and the builder ran out of capital at ~75% complete. No refinance was available at current rates.
- **Current owner / borrower.** Single-asset LLC controlled by an experienced local builder; **cooperative**, equity effectively wiped, motivated to avoid a contested foreclosure on his record.
- **Holder of the distress.** Regional bank **special-assets group**; wants certainty and the asset removed from the book before quarter-end; not a homebuilder and does not want to complete.
- **Why actionable now.** Lender's quarter-end timing + cooperative borrower + our origination of the lead through a brokerage relationship = a short, real window.
- **Why others can't / won't act.** The two mechanic's liens and the "finish a $7.5M home" execution risk deter generalist buyers; that friction is what discounts the note.

## 3. The Asset

- **Description.** ~5,800 SF; 5 bed / 6.5 bath; ~80' of deep-water frontage with ocean access (no fixed bridges); pool, dock, summer kitchen designed; high-end finish package specified.
- **Submarket.** Rio Vista Isles — one of Fort Lauderdale's most desirable waterfront enclaves; consistent trophy-home demand; finished deep-water product is scarce.
- **Construction status (~75%).** Complete: foundation, structure, roof, windows/exterior envelope, rough plumbing/electrical/HVAC. Remaining: interior finishes, millwork/cabinetry, stone, fixtures, pool/dock completion, landscaping, final inspections.
- **Permits & entitlements.** Building permit active; no entitlement risk; CO path is finish-out and final inspections only.
- **Dormancy.** Stalled ~5 months; secured and dried-in; no material deterioration; envelope intact.

## 5. Control Strategy

- **Chosen structure & why.** **Note purchase** is optimal: it's the fastest certain entry, it's senior/secured the moment we close, and it gives us *both* exit ramps to title (consensual DIL or foreclosure) without bidding against anyone at auction.
- **Path to control.** (1) Close note assignment at $3.70M; (2) negotiate liens in parallel; (3) execute deed-in-lieu with the cooperative borrower for fee title; target **~4–6 weeks** to title.
- **Legal mechanics.** Note/mortgage assignment + estoppel; DIL agreement with borrower release; title commitment conditioned on lien resolution — all handled by in-house counsel and title.
- **Downside fallback.** If DIL stalls, **foreclose on the purchased note and credit-bid** — we already hold the senior secured position, so control is not contingent on borrower goodwill.
- **Certainty & speed.** **High.** We hold the senior position at close; the only variable is consensual vs. foreclosed title.
