## 1. Executive Summary

Breakwater Capital Partners ("Breakwater") is a vertically integrated **special situations development platform** that acquires, recapitalizes, restructures, completes, and exits distressed luxury residential development projects in South Florida's premier waterfront submarkets.

We target projects where prior execution has failed — stalled spec homes, defaulted construction loans, incomplete waterfront developments, estate-owned and receiver-controlled assets — and we capture the **completion spread**: the value created by taking a 50–90% complete asset with a broken capital stack and a stranded developer, and delivering a finished, marketable, certificate-of-occupancy luxury home into a supply-constrained market.

The platform's edge is structural, not opportunistic. Most distressed buyers can *acquire* but cannot *finish* — they lack the legal, title, debt, brokerage, and construction machinery required to resolve liens, restructure debt, complete construction, and exit cleanly. Breakwater controls all of it under a single sponsor.

**The ask for capital partners:** participate in a disciplined, asset-by-asset program of senior-secured and preferred-equity special situations investments, underwritten to a defined acquisition box, with a clear path to a discretionary fund as the track record compounds.

## 2. The Opportunity

### 2.1 Why distress is being created in luxury residential — now

| Driver | Mechanism | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Construction-loan maturity defaults** | Short-term construction/bridge loans written in a low-rate environment are maturing into higher rates with no takeout. | Lenders holding non-performing paper on unfinished collateral. |
| **Cost inflation / budget blowouts** | Materials and labor inflation stranded spec builders who priced jobs years ago. | Projects out of money at 60–85% complete. |
| **Over-leveraged / undercapitalized developers** | Thin sponsors who cannot fund completion or a capital call. | Forced sellers, willing JV partners, deed-in-lieu candidates. |
| **Estate, probate & partnership disputes** | Owner death, divorce, or partner litigation freezes a project mid-build. | Motivated, non-economic sellers; receiver-controlled assets. |
| **Lender risk-aversion to construction** | Banks do not want to be in the homebuilding business; special-assets desks want assets *off the books*. | Note sales, discounted payoffs, REO dispositions. |

### 2.2 Why the opportunity is mispriced

Distressed luxury development sits in a **structurally inefficient** corner of the market:

- **Illiquid and idiosyncratic** — every asset is a one-off; there is no clearing price.
- **Relationship-sourced** — the best deals never hit a broad market; they move through lenders, receivers, estate attorneys, and other insiders.
- **Completion risk scares generalist capital** — most opportunistic buyers underwrite acquisition but cannot credibly underwrite *finishing*. That fear is priced into the basis.
- **Few credible counterparties** — the universe of buyers who can simultaneously resolve title/lien issues, restructure debt, and complete a $5–10M waterfront home is small.

Breakwater is built to be one of the few. We are paid for solving the exact problem that depresses the price.

## 3. The Strategy

A repeatable, four-stage value chain. The same machine runs on every asset.

```
   SOURCE            CONTROL              COMPLETE            EXIT
   ──────            ───────              ────────            ────
   Lenders        Note acquisition     Construction        Luxury
   Receivers      Pref equity rescue   completion          brokerage
   Estates        JV recap             Lien / title        disposition
   Brokers        DIL / foreclosure    resolution          Construction-
   Developers     REO acquisition      Repositioning       to-perm takeout
```

1. **Source** — proprietary deal flow from lender special-assets desks, receivers, estate/probate counsel, and brokerage relationships. Distress is the funnel.
2. **Control** — acquire the *right point in the capital stack*: the note, a preferred-equity rescue position, a JV recapitalization, or fee title via deed-in-lieu, foreclosure, or REO purchase. Legal and title infrastructure makes us fast and certain.
3. **Complete** — resolve liens and title defects, restructure or retire debt, and finish construction to CO under disciplined development oversight.
4. **Exit** — dispose through in-house luxury brokerage into a supply-constrained retail market, or refinance via construction-to-perm where a hold is warranted.

**The product we sell is certainty of completion. The return we earn is the completion spread.**

## 4. Why It Works — The Completion Spread

The core return driver is the gap between our **all-in basis** and the **finished retail value**:

```
   Completion Spread  =  Finished Retail Value
                         –  Distressed Basis (note / equity / fee acquisition cost)
                         –  Cost to Complete (construction + soft costs + contingency)
                         –  Carry & Closing (debt service, taxes, insurance, legal, title)
                         –  Disposition Cost
```

Three forces widen that spread in our favor:

1. **Distressed entry** — we acquire below replacement cost because completion risk and capital-stack complexity scare other buyers.
2. **Scarce finished product** — there is no new waterfront land; finished luxury waterfront homes are supply-constrained and price-resilient.
3. **Internalized friction** — capabilities others pay third parties for (legal, title, brokerage, debt) are captured in-house, converting cost drag into retained margin.

The strategy is *not* a bet on market appreciation. Returns are engineered from basis and execution. Appreciation, if it comes, is upside — not the thesis.

## 6. The Moat — Total Vertical Integration

"Anyone can buy a distressed asset cheap" is the objection. The answer is that buying is the easy part — **resolving, finishing, and presenting** is where 90% of distressed buyers fail. Breakwater is *totally vertical*: it controls — or partners to control — every link from the note to the finished, furnished home. The founder's combined licensure provides the in-house spine; strategic build-and-design partnerships complete the chain.

**In-house capabilities** (founder-controlled):

| Capability | What it does | Edge it creates |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal execution** (FL real estate attorney) | Foreclosure, workout, structuring, lien and title litigation handled in-house. | Speed and certainty; no outside-counsel drag or delay; credibility with lenders and receivers. |
| **Title & closing** (title agency owner) | Instant title/lien diligence; clean, controlled closings. | De-risks the single most dangerous part of distressed acquisition; captures title revenue; closes when others can't. |
| **Brokerage** (FL real estate broker) | Off-market sourcing and in-house disposition. | Proprietary deal flow; eliminates 5–6% disposition drag; real-time pricing intelligence. |
| **Debt placement** (mortgage loan originator) | Recap financing and construction-to-perm takeouts. | Optimizes the capital stack; enables hold/refi optionality; lender relationships seed the funnel. |
| **Development oversight** | Manages completion to CO. | Converts a stalled asset into finished, salable product — the part competitors can't execute. |
| **Capital structuring** | Note, pref equity, JV, DIL, foreclosure, REO. | Enters at the optimal point in the stack for each situation. |

**Build & design capabilities** (strategic partnerships — extending control through completion and presentation):

| Capability | What it does | Edge it creates |
|---|---|---|
| **Architecture** (partner) | Re-engineers stalled plans for cost, marketability, and speed to CO. | Value-add by redesign, not default completion; resolves the design flaws that often stalled the project. |
| **General contracting** (partner) | Delivers the build under tightly scoped, accountable contracts (GMP where possible). | Pricing certainty and schedule control; a disciplined build, not a rescued-subcontractor scramble; aligned, repeat-relationship execution. |
| **Interior design & staging** (partner) | Finishes and presents the home to the target buyer's eye. | Maximizes exit price and compresses time on market — the difference between selling the house and selling the lifestyle. |

**Net effect:** Breakwater is the rare counterparty that can underwrite, acquire, clear, finish, *furnish,* and sell — and is therefore the natural *first call* for any holder of a problem asset. Total vertical integration is the moat, and the moat is what makes the returns repeatable rather than lucky. (Note: partnership capabilities are governed by arm's-length agreements with clear scope, pricing, and conflict disclosure — see Risk.)

## 8. Risk — Identified First, Then Mitigated

Consistent with an institutional underwriting culture, we lead with risk.

| Risk | Mitigant |
|---|---|
| **Completion / cost overrun** | Conservative completion budgets with 10–15% contingency; GMP or tightly managed contracts; staged funding tied to inspection; in-house development oversight. |
| **Title / lien / mechanic's lien exposure** | In-house title agency and counsel diligence *before* close; acquisition structures (note, foreclosure, DIL) chosen to deliver clean title; lien resolution as a core competency. |
| **Market / exit risk** | Entry basis sized to finished value with margin of safety; short hold limits exposure; in-house brokerage for real-time repricing and accelerated sale. |
| **Entitlement / permitting** | Prioritize assets with permits in place and vertical construction underway (the 50–90% box); legal diligence on open approvals. |
| **Liquidity / capital-stack** | Conservative leverage; debt placed with maturity headroom; construction-to-perm optionality to avoid forced sales. |
| **Sponsor / key-man** | Document and systematize every function; build a repeatable institutional process (this platform) rather than founder-dependent one-offs. |
| **Legal / regulatory conflicts** | Clear separation and disclosure across the founder's attorney, broker, and title roles; independent counsel where conflicts arise. |

The acquisition box itself is the first risk control: we decline anything outside it.

## 9. How Capital Partners Participate

Because **capital is the current binding constraint**, the platform is structured to make participation simple, secured, and aligned today — and scalable tomorrow.

**Phase 1 — Deal-by-deal (now).** Capital partners co-invest into single-asset special opportunities, each underwritten to the box and presented with its own acquisition memorandum. Positions are structured for downside protection:
- **Senior / note positions** — secured, current or accruing, lower in the return range.
- **Preferred equity** — fixed preferred return plus a profit share, ahead of common.
- **JV common / recap** — full completion-spread participation with Breakwater as operating sponsor.

**Alignment.** Breakwater (GP) co-invests alongside partners and is compensated on performance through a structured waterfall (preferred return to capital, then promote). The GP makes money when partners make money.

**Phase 2 — Programmatic capital.** A committed co-invest line or pledge facility from one or more capital partners, deployed across deals as they clear the box — moving from one-off syndication to repeatable execution.

**Phase 3 — Discretionary fund.** A closed-end special situations / development-recovery fund, seeded by the Phase 1–2 track record and operating infrastructure.
